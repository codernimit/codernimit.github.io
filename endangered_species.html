So what is loss of habitat and genetic variation and how are humans causing it?

How Humans Are Causing Loss Of Habitat and genetic variation

Human activities are causing loss of habitat and genetic variation by taking up space and reducing the population. One way that loss of habitat is making species endangered is by clearing the land for urban use and logging. One example is that the Amazon rainforest is shrinking because people are clearing the land, to “clear” land is to remove all its vegetation from it. Genetic variation means the diversity found within the species. One example of this is that humans are reducing the population by over hunting and fishing so there are less breeding pairs because to make a healthy offspring, you need 2 of the same kind but with barely any similarities in their genes(DNA). If we continue this, what will happen to us in the future?

What are the effects?

The effects will not be good on us if we continue this. “That sounds about right: drill, mine, log and pollute today, stick someone with the bill later on, Unfortunately “Later on” is now,” says The Wilderness society. As human development goes deeper into the wild, we’re experiencing new viruses because more animals are coming near our areas. According to The Wilderness Society, “As human development pushes deeper into formerly “wild” areas, people are exposed to more new disease vectors(a major share of emerging infectious diseases are the kind that jump from animals to people).” According to The Wilderness Society, “Studies of the Amazon have shown that deforestation increases fire risk by removing natural vegetation cover creating drier conditions.” Because of the problems we’re causing, we should save these species.
